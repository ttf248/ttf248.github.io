The multifaceted impacts of the family planning policy: profound changes from social structure to economic and political spheres

China’s family planning policy, while limiting population growth, curbed the development of family-based enterprises, undermined traditional social structures, and suppressed the expansion of family businesses and political influence. Compared to South Korea’s chaebols and India’s family monopolies, its uniqueness is evident. Now that birth restrictions have been lifted, although challenges such as low birth rates are faced, new monopoly risks must also be guarded against, and a balance needs to be sought through multiple avenues.

Jiangxi Province's Special Post and National Civil Servant Teacher Recruitment Data (2020-2025): Trends and Insights into Change

The 2020-2025 teacher recruitment in Jiangxi Province shows a significant reduction trend: the number of special post teachers recruited decreased sharply from 6,617 to 32 (a decrease of 99.5%), and national cadre teachers decreased from 11,324 to 2,146 (a decrease of 81.1%). The proportion of core subjects (language, mathematics, English) remains stable but the total number is shrinking; although the proportion of subjects such as music, arts, and physical education has increased, the absolute numbers are limited (e.g., only 2 people were recruited in 2025 for each). Policy changes have implemented “one position reduction, one position replacement” staffing restrictions, with teacher resources shifting towards vocational education and remote areas, leading to a significant reduction in traditional primary and secondary school positions. In 2025, the planned number for some subjects was zero.

The Celestial Realm of Medical Education and the Butterfly Effect of the Dong Xi Ying Incident

The Peking Union “4+4” program (4 years of non-medical undergraduate studies + 4 years of medical doctoral studies) emphasizes interdisciplinary elite training. The Dong Xiying incident in 2025 exposed its reliance on family background (a lineage of medical professionals/researchers) for entry into the program, raising questions about the clarity of her academic credentials and allegations of plagiarism in her thesis. This incident revealed a contradiction between the elitism of the recruitment model and fairness, while issues regarding shortened training periods and supervised practice remain unresolved.
