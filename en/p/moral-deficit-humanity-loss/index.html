Here’s the English translation:

“To do things properly, first and foremost, you need to have a clear conscience. This allows you to sleep soundly, without minor ailments or major issues. It’s best to avoid making mistakes in the first place. If you do make a mistake, it should be about trying to rectify it, not concealing it or hoping to forget it yourself – humans are also capable of excellent memory. Inner peace is the destination; being able to answer your own questions with integrity allows you to live more lightly.”

Transcript

I have eight words to say that are quite heavy – I feel we’re currently experiencing a moral deficit, a depletion of human nature, and a logical cycle where problems arise and need to be solved, ultimately resolved completely. You need patience to wait for this process of upheaval. For a country like China, many things are a slow process of sorting out. So don’t be pessimistic about change; it’s undergoing a cleansing/reshuffle.

Recently, China has been battling two typhoons – one is intangible and the other is tangible. The intangible typhoon is the vaccine, which is impacting our inner security dam. The other typhoon hasn’t frequently landed in Shanghai, and hasn’t caused Beijing and Tianjin to suffer; it’s a rare event for three directly-administered cities to face a storm together that hasn’t occurred in decades. This is off-topic. What you’re going to be thinking about next is whether your own self-improvement and significant changes – having many answers – will make you happy if the surrounding environment remains unchanged. I have eight words to express this heavily: we currently suffer from a moral deficit and a loss of humanity, which is the biggest deficit and greatest loss.

Transcript

However, people were expecting the safety risks of vaccines to be completely resolved, just like with the initial formula scandal. So sometimes you need to look at history and understand that you know about the establishment and formation of a complete legal framework for the US Food and Drug Administration, which is closely related to the initial unsafe formula and dairy products. Therefore, the Sanlu milk powder incident forced China to undergo significant changes in the dairy industry. While vaccines aren’t going to be one after another, I hope this time it’s a halt. You need to know that times always move forward through a logical cycle of problems being identified and completely resolved, otherwise what do journalists do? What do citizens do? I think we can all do is care. But the problem is that Chinese people are too easily forgetful. “As I just said, after hitting someone’s car and then speeding off the side of the road, a whole vehicle of people didn’t stop to intervene. A large proportion of our neighbors and colleagues are like that – people who behave this way. So we need to gradually change that. And as ordinary citizens, the things we can do is pay attention, not forget. I don’t think it’s a lack of something; when you’re hungry and cold, and you spend your days talking about ideals, it doesn’t have any persuasive power. But once they’ve eaten and are warm, if they let themselves become the world’s number one country for diabetes, number one for hypertension, and so many other ‘number one’ countries… then you’ll see Chinese people starting to run and start dieting. And when you ask the ladies in this audience, who hasn’t experienced the brutal experience of not being able to lose weight because I couldn’t afford a decent meal?”

Transcript

The spiritual aspect we’ve been discussing is also the same. When you’re well-fed and warm, and start running or dieting, your need for spirit will gradually increase as well. For example, I used to smoke, but one day after starting to run, I suddenly realized I hadn’t smoked in 20 days – not at all. I haven’t smoked since. Of course, I wouldn’t deliberately say, “I’ve completely quit!” because I think it lacks a certain ceremony; it doesn’t rule out smoking two or three cigarettes during the year. When your lifestyle changes, many things will change along with it. I believe that for Chinese people, you need to be patient and wait for the upheaval. More and more people feel unhappy and unwilling to indulge themselves in unhappiness. “While depression is increasing, on the other hand, isn’t it also seeing more and more people seeking positive ways to live? At this time, things related to the mental aspect will grow, so don’t be pessimistic; how you look at the same thing matters. I often see people cutting in line while driving, and I feel frustrated, but then I quickly become optimistic because the queue on the other side is longer than before. This is a process like this – all of you, gathered here tonight after the rain has stopped, willingly engaging in so much pointless conversation… isn’t that a wonderful thing?”

Transcript

This is also a transformation, so there are many things that need to be thought about in a different way. Of course, I think there will be much to learn in the future, such as when you talk about starting a business – is everyone an entrepreneur? Everyone’s life is an entrepreneurial one. We’re giving too much of a time-sensitive element to entrepreneurship, but entrepreneurship has always been around. Wasn’t Su Shi (Su Dongpo) an entrepreneur? Wasn’t Li Bai an entrepreneur? Everyone will have many innovations throughout their lives, and ultimately they created their own brand. It doesn’t matter if most innovations don’t come to fruition, as long as you live a life that is rich in flavor and you believe it’s worth it. I think China particularly lacks good failure as another value of success. People only accept successful outcomes, but a good failure is also a success, and we don’t accept that. “I think that’s going to be very difficult, so you see why Chinese football isn’t played well has many reasons, and one of those reasons is that everyone is afraid to lose the ball after passing it to someone else – they just let it go. If there wasn’t this kind of play, of course, that’s only a very small part of the reason. So I think as I get to my age, by 30, it’s hard to write about ‘Transparent Happiness’ and can’t be rushed, and now at 50, there’s a bit of urgency because time is passing so quickly, and you expect many things that haven’t become reality. But on the other hand, I also understand that for a country like China, many things are a slow shuffling process. You see older generations – some were completely oblivious to anything, but occasionally you’ll see children pulling their fathers along as they change. They’re changing, they’re being shuffled around, so I think you need to be patient.”
