Chinas good future will be one with kind elders

I hope that in twenty years, I’ll be a lovely old man, accompanied by a lovely old woman. Not seeking great wealth or status, just good health and the ability to travel around.

Feb 15, 2020
3 minute read

Script

I’m going to work hard to become a lovable old man. The China of the future will be one with good old men and women. Even if I’m 60 years old in 10 years, I’ll still be among the youngest in this world.

To be honest, people find it quite impressive that a 50-year-old Chinese man can have this physique. But behind this body is the principle I believe in: the more disciplined you are, the freer you become! After the rain stopped, I wanted to go running, and I’ll play soccer tomorrow afternoon. At 50, I can still compete at a high level – I’m not kidding, often playing with professionals. But what’s behind it? Discipline. I spend my remaining days running, and some find it monotonous. The more disciplined you are, the freer you become. Because of my discipline, I am free to run, never listening to music; I believe listening to my own breath is the most beautiful music for running.

I rarely use the treadmill. Despite Beijing’s severe smog, I run five days a week—two already reserved for the smog. Unlike others, I never skip a day. Each month, I mark a running day in my journal, always at least 18 days. Runs fly by; I don’t wear glasses while running. More importantly, I also play soccer weekly. My last class with my graduate students will be at my home, focusing on the theme of fun—I believe fun is crucial. I never interact with people lacking joy and hobbies.

To treat them with respect and keep a distance – such people are truly frightening. If you don’t find joy, what joy do you have? I really don’t have much either. What kind of work do you like? This year at the beginning of the year, one of my graduate students was assigned to China News Weekly. We had a special topic. Every year when my graduate students graduated, their last assignment was to write an article about 10 years from now, which I would keep. Then, 10 years later, I would unfold them one by one. I also wrote about turning 50 and writing to 60 – something I never thought would be so distant, beyond the map, but suddenly it’s my next stop. Writing from 20 to 30 for my students is like a love letter from spring to summer. But writing from 50 to 60 is just a murmur from late summer to autumn. Now, I’m writing to 10 years from now – slowly writing about myself, about a bed, a meal, and the people around my family. That’s perfectly normal.

But what kind of person should one be at 60? My goal is clear. As stated in the preface, this article is lengthy. I want to strive to become a lovable old man in 10 years. China’s lovability depends not only on having more young people who will become responsible citizens – no longer just ordinary folks, but energetic and rule-abiding youth – but also on having good old men and women. Currently, over 230 million Chinese are already over 60; in 10 years, that number will certainly exceed 300 million. In terms of the population aged 60 and above alone, China would be a top 5 country globally, possibly even third – just imagine how daunting that is!

I don’t think so. I don’t know if everyone has seen the table online showing the average life expectancy in various Chinese provinces and municipalities; Shanghai and Beijing both exceed 80 years old, counting both men and women. The average lifespan certainly favors men over women. That means even when I’m 60 in ten years, I’ll be among the youngest in this third stage of life. Women retire at 55 to an average age of 80, leaving them with 25 years; men retire at 60 to an average age of 80, leaving them with 20 years! You can’t just do square dancing all the time. And I can say that today’s older sisters and brothers doing square dancing have been given this cultural habit and entertainment by the times.

In another 20 years, there will likely still be square dancing, but on a much smaller scale. Younger generations are not as enthusiastic about group activities; just as older people prefer organized tours, young people increasingly opt for independent and self-guided travel. Square dancing will continue to exist, but its scope will diminish—this isn’t inherently good or bad, it’s simply the times changing. When I was 60, the youngest member of a team, I should be asking myself: what kind of person should I become? Chinese painting says that at 60, your ears are attuned – but I certainly wouldn’t listen to everything happily; I’d still hear what shouldn’t be said, and even happiness can turn sour. More importantly, what can I do for the younger generation?

What can you do to contribute to good things? Don’t be lazy, don’t compromise easily, oppose what needs opposing. Can you stand in front of a young person when they might harm someone’s interests? I often find myself looking at this mirror now. A good friend of mine has passed away. Tao Wei used to gather with us frequently at our home and once told me a true story. We all felt the pain together. The older generation, like Chen Lao, hoarded everything in their homes – you break open a box and find over 30 items hidden under the bed. You tell them this shirt cost over 500 yuan, now it’s being worshipped with incense daily. Therefore, our generation grew accustomed to battling wits with our parents. What’s the value of something bought for 700 yuan?

It’s easy to run into trouble with these things. Once, Tao Wei spent over 400 yuan on a T-shirt for his father. It was a really nice shirt; how much did it cost? 99. I wore that style and the next day, disaster struck. When he came back in the evening, he demanded 400 yuan from Tao Wei to buy four more for me. After wearing mine out today, Uncle Li and others all praised it. They said this kind of behavior carries a significant risk. Don’t do such things when you’re older. I won’t mention specifics about who is in the hospital orthopedic ward – an elderly person fractured their bone, and the crucial reason was the shoes bought from a street vendor. Of course, this only addresses the material aspect. Even as we age, we need spiritual life, curiosity, and a willingness to shield younger generations from hardship. Live each day happily.

I’m curious and excited about turning 60, feeling it will be a wonderful beginning

Thank you all
