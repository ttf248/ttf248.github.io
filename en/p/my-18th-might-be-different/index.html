The very common phrase information fragmentation – since graduating from high school, aside from the time I spent reading novels, I haven’t really taken the time to sit down and read a book quietly and attentively for a long time. Sometimes, looking back, after so many years working, I still remember what I did each year? Often, by the middle of the year, I’ve forgotten a lot of things from the first half. Writing a blog is a good habit, even though much of what I write isn’t appropriate and doesn’t matter – it was originally written for myself to read.

My most loyal readers are myself.

Transcript

Each of one’s 18 years is like a gaze of expectation and questioning. Each person occasionally takes their own 18th birthday to ask themselves, “Are you the way you wanted to live back then?” I’m afraid that nowadays everyone has countless circles, no friends, chatting all day long, nobody to have a heart-to-heart conversation, knowledge acquisition is endless, but far from wisdom. Everyone should use your 18th birthday to ask yourself, it’s too easy to deceive others, but very difficult to deceive yourself!

I think each of one’s 18 years is like a gaze of expectation and questioning. You can deceive others, but you cannot deceive your 18-year-old self. Is today you the way you were going to be at 18? I think it’s okay. - Today, material fame and countless other things are valued far more than what I, at 18, could have imagined. However, on the other hand, we’re always moving forward. When I was 18, I was studying journalism at the Broadcasting Academy, and I wanted to become like Farquhar – the best journalist. Today, I’m still on that path, which is why many people say, “How can White still be on CCTV?” because I want to be a good journalist. The pursuit of news continues. This is my 18-year-old perspective, so I think everyone should occasionally ask themselves: Are you the person you originally wanted to be?

Transcript

This is something you can’t fool anyone with. This is what I looked like at 18, and it’s just gone in an instant – 32 years have passed. Every student who came to Beijing to study at that time would have this same photo. Back then, the crumpled suits on Tianmen Square, the school badge pinned to your chest – because there were so few university students back then, no badges, you felt incredibly proud. We had long hair back then, but we still loved our 18-year-old selves. Looking back after all these years, I suddenly realize that I’m grateful for the things I faced at 18, because they shaped me silently. It was May 8th, 1986, and I bought a selection of Misty Poems at Wangfujing Bookstore that year. And today, I suddenly realized that my writing style has been most influenced by Cui Jian’s “One Evening,” which I heard at the Workers’ Stadium. It’s had the biggest impact on me – including the fact that my personality was shaped by Misty Poetry, rock music, and Lu Guofu’s martial arts novels.

Transcript

In 1966, the Cultural Revolution ended after 76 years of defeating the Gang of Four – it was largely accidental. It could only be truly achieved through the enlightenment and awakening of human nature and the growth of every individual. I believe we still need to go through a period of enlightenment and fully understand human nature. Regardless of how much economic progress China makes, if we don’t genuinely fill in this understanding of humanity – then guide it, control its darker side, and activate its good side – there will continue to be many things to worry about in the future. So my 18th year was also representative of that era; it went too far, but we shouldn’t forget why we started out. It’s now been condensed into these four words: “Don’t Lose Your Original Intent.” So, I think regardless of how far you go, everyone should ask themselves what their 18-year-old self would think. It’s good to keep a photo from that time and often ask yourself, “What do others say? Does it matter?” It’s much easier to fool others than to fool yourself. I just said to a 20-year-old today, based on my own 18 years, you should always let your 18-year-old be the eyes watching you. And as I also mentioned earlier, in your twenties, go try many doors – you don’t know which one is right for you. It’s surprising in China now to often see older people in their twenties and lots of younger people in their seventies.

Thank you everyone.
