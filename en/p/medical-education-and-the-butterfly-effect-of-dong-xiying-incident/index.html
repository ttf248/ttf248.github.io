Harmony 4+4: Deconstructing Medical Education’s “Tian Gong”

The “4+4” project at the Beijing Union Medical College, since its launch in 2018, has been viewed as China’s “Tian Gong” – or celestial palace – in medical education. This project, designed to mimic the American medical education system, adopted the philosophy of “cultivating multidisciplinary backgrounds, nurturing love for medicine, and attracting outstanding talents from across the nation.” It aimed to create a rift within traditional Chinese medical education by: replacing the traditional 5+3+3 (five-year bachelor’s degree + three-year master’s degree + three-year doctoral degree) model with a four-year non-medical undergraduate program plus a four-year medical doctorate training. This was intended to cultivate cross-disciplinary leadership talent in medicine.

This project’s “Tian Gong” attributes are reflected across three dimensions:

  1. Elite Recruitment: Early requirements stipulated that applicants must come from QS/Times/US News ranked top 50 universities or US News’s top 10 liberal arts colleges (such as Barnard College), later relaxed to the top 100, but still requiring a GPA of 3.6 or being within the top 30% of their class. Internationalization of Curriculum Pathways: Adopting an organ-system integration curriculum in the American style, PBL (Problem-Based Learning) methodology, students must complete core courses such as anatomy and pathology within 4 years, and participate in clinical internships.

  2. Controversy Regarding Export Quality: Despite a slightly lower pass rate for practitioners’ examinations (95.2%) compared to the traditional eight-year program (98.5%), graduates predominantly enter affiliated hospitals, and their career development shows no significant difference from traditional pathways.

Dong Xiying Incident: A “Celestial Realm” Crack Revealed by Ordinary Eyes

In April 2025, the extramarital affair involving Xiao Fei, a thoracic surgeon at the China-Japan Friendship Hospital, triggered scrutiny of the “4+4 Project” of the Traditional Chinese Medicine Hospital of Capital Medical University, commonly known as “Covelong 4+4.” The background of Dong Xiying, the key figure involved, sparked three major questions:

  1. Ambiguity Regarding Educational Background:
    • Dispute Over Undergraduate Institution: Dong Xiying claimed to have graduated from Columbia University, but she actually attended Barnard College (ranked 14th in US News & World Report for liberal arts colleges in 2025). Although the college met Covelong’s requirement of “top 20 liberal arts colleges,” her diploma simultaneously listed “Barnard College” and “Columbia University,” which could easily be misinterpreted as Columbia University’s main campus in the domestic recruitment market.
    • Dispute Over Professional Field: An economics undergraduate degree combined with a clinical medicine doctorate, while aligning with the 4+4 project’s positioning of “multi-disciplinary background,” has been questioned for lacking a foundation in pre-clinical medical courses.

Dong Xi Ying Incident: Peering Through the “Heavenly Palace” Crack – A Glimpse for Ordinary People

  • Unique Family Background:

    • Academic Resource Monopoly: Her father was the secretary of the China Metallurgical and Research Bureau Construction Institute, her mother was a deputy director of the North Medical University Engineering Technology Research Institute, and her grandfather was an academician in the field of medical imaging. Her paternal grandparents were doctoral supervisors (Doyens) at North Medical University in the field of materials. This “medicine + research + university” composite family background provided implicit resources such as recommendation letters (requiring recommendations from two biomedical associate professors) and research collaborations (such as North Medical University patents).
    • Career Path Leaps: She transitioned from cardiac surgery during residency to urology, with research spanning orthopedics, gastroenterology, radiology, and other fields. This was questioned as “paved road” due to the rapid changes in her career path.

  • Exaggerated Dispute Over Study Duration:

    • Reasonableness of Time Compression: Traditional medical doctorates require 11 years (5+3+3), while the 4+4 program compressed the time to 8 years. Despite Harmony emphasizing “curriculum integration” and “early clinical exposure,” some physicians pointed out that its clinical internship period (12 months) is still less than the traditional eight-year program (18 months), potentially impacting skill accumulation.

  • The Controversy Regarding CCT Reduction: Some CCT bases allowing 4+4 graduates to waive one year of CCT are being questioned for undermining industry fairness.

The Medical Education Quagmire Behind the Controversy

The Dong Xiying incident reflects deep contradictions within Chinese medical education:

  1. Conflict between Elitism and Equity:

    • The “application-assessment” system of the 4+4 program is essentially a transplantation of the American medical school model, but China lacks standardized exams like MCAT, relying instead on subjective evaluations such as recommendations and research experience, which are significantly influenced by family background.
    • Data shows that among graduates of the 4+4 program in 2023, 30% came from Qingbei Fuzhou (the top universities), and 25% from overseas prestigious schools. The influence of family background on applications is significant.

  2. The Reality of Curriculum Reform:

    • The Cost of Time Compression: 4+4 students produce significantly fewer research outputs (such as the number of publications) than traditional eight-year programs, and are accused of “prioritizing clinical work over research.”
    • Divergence in Industry Recognition: Three-tier hospitals prioritize the reputation of Covance (the name of the top university), while基层医院 (jīchéng yīyuàn - primary hospitals) maintain a cautious attitude towards the clinical abilities of 4+4 graduates. - The Confusion Surrounding the International Integration of Medical Education:
  • The US 4+4 model requires passing the MCAT exam and completing a 3-7 year residency, while China’s 4+4 programs have simplified the entry and residency stages, leading to “form resembling but not truly similar.”
  • Data from the Ministry of Education shows that in 2024, 4+4 programs accounted for less than 5% of national medical doctorate admissions, and their large-scale promotion still faces resistance.

Conclusion: Reconstructing the “Tiangong” Order of Medical Education

The controversy surrounding the CoHo 4+4 project fundamentally represents a clash between elite education and public expectations. When a corner of the “Tiangong” (Heavenly Palace) was glimpsed by mortals, it exposed not just isolated instances of flaws, but deeper contradictions within the entire medical education system. Future reforms must focus on three levels:

  1. Transparent Selection Mechanisms: Introduce standardized exams (such as a Chinese version of MCAT) to reduce the influence of subjective factors like letters of recommendation.
  2. Extend Clinical Training Cycle: Restore the clinical training cycle for 4+4 graduates to 3 years, ensuring that clinical competence is achieved.
  3. Strengthen Process Oversight: Establish an academic integrity review mechanism and conduct rigorous originality verification for interdisciplinary research.

Medical education’s “Tiangong” should not be a privilege reserved for a select few, but rather a true cradle for nurturing medical elites.
