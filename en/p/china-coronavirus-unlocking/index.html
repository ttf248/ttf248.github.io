China's COVID-19 lifting of restrictions

The policy was announced very suddenly and implemented quickly; health codes are no longer required, nor are they checked at public venues

Browsing the New York Times Chinese website, the entire layout is discussing China reopening

Dec 22, 2022

The policy was announced very suddenly and implemented quickly; health codes are no longer required, nor are they checked at public venues

Browsing the New York Times Chinese website, the entire layout is discussing China reopening

Do not evaluate policies, just record what is happening around you

Beijing never had a “clearance” in the first place. With the opening of restrictions, it spread rapidly, with widespread infections. Among my friends, no one has experienced severe illness.

Shenzhen, bordering Guangzhou, has also begun to develop rapidly; I’m based in Shanghai, but due to my company being near the suburbs, there hasn’t been widespread infection yet when I’m writing this

The protective measures in my hometown were inadequate, leading to widespread diffusion afterward

It feels roughly the same as before, suddenly loosening up, with a new policy every week until it was fully opened

It can’t be denied that the three-year zero-COVID policy had an effect, fostering a sense of acceptance and going with the flow
