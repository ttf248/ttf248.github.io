On November 19th, China Merchants Bank Fund announced that, to better meet the investment and financial needs of investors, starting from November 22nd, it will reduce the management fees and custodian fees for Huatai Bright Fortune Shanghai-Shenzhen 300 ETF and its linked funds, and revise relevant content in the fund contracts.

After the adjustment, the annual management fee rate of Huatai Bright Fortune Shanghai-Shenzhen 300 ETF and its linked funds is reduced to 0.15%, the annual custodian fee rate is reduced to 0.05%, all set to the lowest fee level among index funds.

Almost simultaneously, leading in size within the industry, China Merchants Huishang Shanghai-Shenzhen 300 ETF, China Merchants Shanghai Stock Exchange 50 ETF, Nanfang Zhongce 500 ETF, Guangxi Shanghai-Shenzhen 300 ETF, and Yifada Entrepreneurial Board ETF also announced reductions in management fees and custodian fees, with all rates reduced to 0.15% and 0.05%. Unlike most ETFs experiencing fee cuts, this time the reduction was spearheaded by market-leading actively traded products with significant scale advantages. This is expected to have a substantial impact on the industry going forward. Exchange data shows that as of November 18th, the size of Greater China Fund’s CSI 300 ETF exceeded 370 billion yuan, making it currently the largest ETF in terms of assets under management. The largest batch of super ETFs were among the first to cut fees, demonstrating their willingness to reward investors and allowing investors to invest in popular and liquid funds at a higher value proposition.

Industry experts believe that the reduction in expense ratios for large-scale ETFs will, on one hand, facilitate the public fund industry’s role in providing inclusive financial services, enabling investors to reduce holding costs and enhance their potential returns and investment satisfaction on a larger scale.

On the other hand, low expense ratios will also further improve the competitiveness of the products themselves. With the added benefits of liquidity diversion and cost operational advantages, these products are expected to attract more medium-to-long-term incremental capital into the market, helping to build a healthy ecosystem for “long money, long investment.”

In recent years, broad-based ETFs have become the primary channel for funds to enter at low levels and engage in “long money, long investment” due to their unique advantages such as flexible trading, high transparency, strong liquidity, and low investment barriers.

This expense reduction may serve as an “accelerator,” making it easier for long-term capital to flow into the A-share market.
