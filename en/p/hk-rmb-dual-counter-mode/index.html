The Hong Kong Exchanges and Clearing (HKEX) announced on December 13th that it will soon launch the “Hong Kong Dollar-Renminbi Dual-Listing Mode” (hereinafter referred to as “Dual-Listing Mode”) and dual-listing broker mechanisms for its securities markets, further supporting RMB-designated listings, trading, and settlement in Hong Kong.

Dual-Listing Model and Dual-Listing Dealer Mechanism

Hong Kong Exchanges and Clearing (HKEX) stated that the registration process for these new measures is expected to commence in the first half of 2023, pending regulatory approval and market readiness. Under the dual-listing model, HKEX will optimize related trading and settlement arrangements, allowing investors to swap securities issued by the same issuer in both Hong Kong Dollar (HKD) and Renminbi (RMB) trading venues.

To enhance liquidity of the RMB trading venue and narrow the price gap between the two venues, HKEX will introduce a dual-listing dealer mechanism. Once relevant legislation is passed by the Legislative Council, market dealers engaged in providing quoted liquidity will be exempt from stamp duty when conducting specific transactions. Simultaneously, these new measures will also prepare for subsequent mainland investors to trade securities priced in RMB through the Hong Kong Connect trading scheme. “The launch of the Hong Kong-Renminbi (RMB) dual currency clearing arrangement and the dual currency dealing mechanism for institutional investors is a key measure to support market development. In conjunction with our other market initiatives, this arrangement will help attract more dual currency securities listed in Hong Kong and leverage synergies with existing Mainland products on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange. ‘The Hong Kong Exchanges and Clearing (HKEX) is committed to actively promoting the internationalization of RMB and continuously enhancing Hong Kong’s position as a leading offshore RMB center globally,’ said Mr. Yao Kai-yan, Chief Operating Officer and Head of Market Liaison. It is noted that the current listing, trading, settlement and clearing arrangements in the Hong Kong stock market will largely apply to RMB clearing securities under the dual currency clearing arrangement. HKEX will announce the implementation date of the dual currency clearing model and the list of eligible dual currency securities included in the institutional investor mechanism at an appropriate time.”

How to Identify Hong Kong Dollar-Renminbi Trading Booths

Hong Kong Exchange documents show that the Hong Kong dollar-Renminbi dual listing arrangement will largely follow the existing stock code allocation plan, namely that stocks on the Hong Kong dollar booth will have 5-digit stock codes starting with “0,” while stocks on the Renminbi booth will have 5-digit stock codes starting with “8.” The last four digits of stock codes for both the Hong Kong dollar and Renminbi booths will be identical. The short names of stocks on the Renminbi booth will be appended with “-R.”

Regarding trading arrangements, based on securities that are the same category and can be converted between the Renminbi and Hong Kong dollar booths, if one booth (such as the Hong Kong dollar booth) is a designated security that can be shorted, the other booth (such as the Renminbi booth) can also be included as a designated security that can be shorted in accordance with exchange rules. Correspondingly, both booths will appear on the list of designated securities that can be shorted published by the exchange. Given that the stocks on two trading desks belong to the same category and can be mutually converted, buying or holding them in Hong Kong dollars and selling them in Renminbi will be treated as shorting the position, and vice versa. The settlement time between the two desks is T+2.

For designated stocks eligible for short selling, such as borrowing shares in Hong Kong dollars and then selling them on the Renminbi desk, this will be considered secured short selling, and vice versa.

It’s worth noting that under the dual-desk model, since the Renminbi desk is only for trading and settlement, it will not provide physical stock deposits or withdrawals; physical stocks can only be deposited after being converted to the Renminbi desk, and similarly, the Renminbi desk must be converted to the Hong Kong dollar desk before physical stocks can be withdrawn.

How to Identify Hong Kong Dollar-Renminbi Trading Service Desks

Fees associated with related settlement and clearing of transactions, all Hong Kong settlement fees (excluding dividend collection service fee and interest collection service fee), are calculated and charged in Hong Kong Dollars. Dividend collection service fee and interest collection service fee are calculated in the eligible currency used by the relevant securities.
