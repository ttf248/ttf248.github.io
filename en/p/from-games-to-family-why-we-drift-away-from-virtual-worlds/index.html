From Games to Family: Why We Gradually Drift Away from Virtual Worlds as We Grow Up

What were the happiest times playing games – sneaking to play Tank Battle, Super Mario, and Contra on our family’s TV as a child, or secretly playing Age of Empires on our family’s computer with my cousin, or later in college, playing Devil May Cry and League of Legends with my roommates?

Childhood had no worries, and when you played, you were completely absorbed in it – a level of immersion that’s impossible to find again as an adult

As times change and society progresses, we who were once active in virtual worlds are gradually realizing that the game platform filled with joy and competition seems to be drifting away from our lives. The disconnection of game social links, accompanied by players’ gradual departure, is not only due to technological advancements and the replacement of games themselves, but also because of a shift in our life’s focus. As we grow older, we become busy with marriage and having children, and the main thread of life gradually shifts from personal entertainment to family responsibilities. Today, let’s discuss the underlying reasons behind this trend.

The Disappearance of Gaming Socialization: A Tug-of-War Between Virtual and Real Worlds

For many, games weren’t just entertainment during their youth; they were a social outlet. Popular online multiplayer games offered challenges and competition while connecting players worldwide, creating virtual communities. Playing dungeons and team battles with friends was part of life back then, as were late-night voice chats and sharing daily experiences – memories we cherish.

However, with time, more and more players have found that this virtual social experience lacks lasting appeal. As games are updated and new platforms emerge, players increasingly find more interactive ways to socialize outside of gaming. In-game friendships gradually fade from view, and many previously active social connections within the game eventually dissolve.

Games and Responsibility: A Shift in Life’s Focus After Adulthood

Entering adulthood, responsibilities gradually increase. Many young people once invested significant time in games, but with the start of their careers or shared living/family formation, these previously essential leisure activities are squeezed by life’s obligations. Marriage, having children, and even caring for parents – these major responsibilities require choices regarding energy and time.

Gaming has become a thing of the past, replaced by the prioritization of family life. Many young people who once “struggled” in games are gradually realizing that real-life is the most important matter. Especially after having children, the focus of their lives shifts completely to family, and gaming is pushed to the periphery.

From Games to Family: How Changes in Life Affect Our Entertainment Choices

In our life experiences, family is one of the most important components. Once entering family life, time and energy become more specific and limited. To care for children and spend time with their partners, many young parents find they no longer have enough time to focus on games they once loved. Sometimes, they even lose interest in game updates and new releases. Gradually, social media platforms and offline interactions with friends and family become the preferred way for many people to socialize.

This doesn’t mean we’ve completely abandoned entertainment, but rather chosen ways more suitable for our current life stage. Many friends who used to connect through games may now maintain contact via other means—family gatherings, meals together, or short trips—due to diverging life paths.

Farewell to the virtual world: How do we view this shift?

For many who once immersed themselves in gaming, saying goodbye to game-related social connections isn’t easy. That virtual world, which brought us joy and a sense of accomplishment, has become part of our memories. However, farewell doesn’t mean severing ties completely; it means making different choices as life changes, balancing our roles in the real world. As we mature from youthful boys to responsible adults, while games still hold value, they can no longer be the main theme of our lives.

In reality, this shift reflects a facet of human adaptability—our ability to adjust our focus and lifestyle according to different stages. When responsibilities and family become central to life, gaming socialization is no longer a priority but rather an occasional pastime.

Future games: may usher in another wave of “sense of belonging.”

Of course, with social development and technological advancements, the forms and content of games are constantly evolving. For example, modern online multiplayer games are increasingly moving towards more relaxed and flexible approaches, and some even prioritize family interaction and parent-child relationships. In the future, we may see more game formats that better integrate into family life and provide players with healthier, more balanced social experiences.

Just as many people still feel nostalgia and passion when talking about “childhood games,” perhaps we, in the future, will also unexpectedly revisit those past games, bringing new understanding and experience, and re-enter that virtual world

Life is changing, but love remains

Whether it’s games or family, life’s changes consistently accompany our growth. We gradually realize that while virtual worlds offer endless joy and excitement, we ultimately return to reality, to the warmest place – home. There’s no need to regret leaving games, as each stage of life has its own unique meaning. These changes are what make us more complete.
