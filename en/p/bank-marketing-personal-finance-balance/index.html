Recently, home renovation projects have significantly increased our daily expenses. We usually use credit cards and pay them off within the billing cycle. Although we have enough cash on hand, we prefer to keep it in money market funds to earn some extra interest income. At the same time, to ensure financial stability, we’ve set up automatic payments so that credit card bills can be paid on time when they are due.

Bank status: Deposits increasing, loans decreasing

Amidst growing economic uncertainty, people are more inclined to save rather than consume or invest. This leads to an increase in bank deposits but also means banks need to pay higher interest rates to depositors. On the other hand, reduced consumption and investment activities lead to lower loan demand, making it difficult for banks to generate interest income through lending.

To attract and retain customers, banks are forced to offer more competitive deposit rates, which further compresses their net interest margin. At the same time, in order to stimulate economic growth and consumption, the central bank may adopt a policy of lowering benchmark interest rates, which will also affect lending rates and subsequently impact banks’ profitability.

Bank marketing strategies: Cultivating user habits

Repayment time is approaching soon. First, Bank of Communications contacted me, offering a one-year installment service with no interest charges. Shortly after that, China Merchants Bank also provided a discount on installment interest at 25%, resulting in an annualized interest rate of just 1.9%. Faced with these offers, I chose to accept the installment services from both banks.

I realized that banks are truly sparing no effort to cultivate user habits. According to the definition of a bank’s clientele, I should be considered a valuable customer. In the current context of difficult loan disbursement for banks, by cultivating my installment mindset, they are essentially preparing for potential future financial difficulties I may encounter, at which point they will be able to earn more interest income from me. After all, it is well known that credit card bill interest rates are not low.

Banks increase credit card usage and build a positive image by offering free installment services and low-interest installment promotions. This shift in strategy demonstrates the bank’s rapid response to market changes and deep understanding of customer needs. In this way, banks not only solve the problem of difficult loan disbursement but also lay the groundwork for future profits—profitability isn’t just about the present; it requires a long-term perspective.

The importance of personal financial management

Although bank installment offers may sound tempting, as users, we should be aware of the risks of over-relying on credit card installments. We should fully consider our repayment ability and future financial needs to avoid falling into long-term debt due to short-term financial convenience. The key to personal financial management lies in balancing current needs with future planning.
