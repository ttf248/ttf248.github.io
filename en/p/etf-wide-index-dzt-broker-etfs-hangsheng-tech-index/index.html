Brokerage ETFs

Securities ETF refers to exchange-traded funds (ETFs) with China’s securities companies as their primary investment target, primarily investing in listed securities company stocks in the Chinese stock market. Securities ETFs typically track a specific securities company index, such as the Zhongxin Securities Company Index.

Hang Seng Technology Index

The Hang Seng Tech Index is a stock index launched by the Hong Kong Stock Exchange, designed to reflect the overall performance of the technology sector in the Hong Kong market. The index includes major tech companies listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange, covering multiple fields such as the internet, e-commerce, cloud computing, and artificial intelligence.

Buy back to cover losses on the left side

The “left-side falling price replenishment” strategy is an investment approach where, during a period of continuous stock price decline, investors anticipate the price nearing a bottoming zone and proactively purchase in batches to reduce holding costs, awaiting future price rebounds for profit

This method falls under the category of “left-side trading,” which means taking a contrarian position before the market has clearly bottomed out. In practice, investors typically employ a “pyramid” accumulation strategy, gradually increasing their purchase quantity as the price declines to reduce their average holding cost. For example, initially buying a certain number of shares; if the price continues to fall, then increasing the buy volume at a lower price, and so on.

Advantages:

  • Reduce holding costs: By buying in batches during a downturn, average stock cost is diluted, creating conditions for future profits

  • Positioning in advance: Establishing a position before the market reverses; if the judgment is correct, greater returns can be achieved when the market recovers

Disadvantages:

  • High risk: Continued decline in stock price may lead to greater losses

  • Funding requirements: Requires sufficient funds to support continuous replenishment, and may face a situation where funds are locked up for an extended period

Relatively speaking, “right-side trading” involves entering buy positions after the market trend is clear and there are obvious signs of price recovery, which carries relatively low risk but may miss some of the gains from the early stages of a rebound. Therefore, investors should choose a suitable trading strategy based on their risk tolerance, financial situation, and market judgment.

Valuation theory

Valuation theory is a core concept in finance, designed to assess the intrinsic value of assets or companies and provide a basis for investment decisions. Through valuation, investors can determine whether the current market price of an asset is reasonable and formulate corresponding investment strategies.

Main valuation methods:

  1. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF): This method is based on the expected future cash flows generated by an asset, which are then discounted to their present value using an appropriate discount rate. The DCF method emphasizes the intrinsic value of an asset and is suitable for evaluating companies with stable and predictable profitability.

  2. Relative Valuation: This method assesses a company’s relative value by comparing its valuation metrics with those of other companies in the same industry, such as price-to-earnings ratio (P/E), price-to-book ratio (P/B), and price-to-sales ratio (P/S). Relative valuation is easy to operate and widely used for quick assessments and comparisons within an industry.

  3. The asset-based approach determines a company’s value by assessing its net asset value, which is the difference between total assets and liabilities. This method is suitable for asset-dominant companies such as real estate firms or capital-intensive businesses.

Application of Valuation Theory

  • Investment decisions: Through valuation, investors can identify undervalued or overvalued assets and formulate buy-or-sell strategies

  • Mergers and Acquisitions: In M&A transactions, valuation is key to determining the transaction price and influencing the success or failure of the deal

  • Financial Report: The company needs to assess its assets and liabilities at fair value to ensure the accuracy and transparency of financial reports

It is important to note that valuation results are affected by various factors, including market conditions, company operating status, and macroeconomic indicators. Therefore, when conducting a valuation, it is necessary to comprehensively consider all factors and combine qualitative and quantitative analysis to obtain more accurate assessment results.
