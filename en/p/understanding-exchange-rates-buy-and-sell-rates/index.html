In the foreign exchange market, particularly at banks or currency exchange points, we often see terms like “buy rate” and “sell rate.” Many people may not be clear on these concepts, or even confuse them. Today, we will use a simple example to help everyone understand the meaning and function of these rates.

What are “buy rate” and “sell rate”?

  • Banks or currency exchange institutions are willing to buy foreign currency at this rate; that is, when you sell foreign currency (like US dollars) to a bank, the bank will pay you in RMB according to the buying rate

  • Banks or currency exchange institutions are willing to sell foreign currency at this rate; that is, when you buy foreign currency with RMB, the bank will sell it to you at the selling rate

In short:

  • The exchange rate at which the bank buys foreign currency from you
  • The price the bank offers you for foreign currency

It’s important to note that banks’ buying and selling exchange rates are typically different, with the selling rate generally higher than the buying rate. This difference is how banks make a profit.

Case Study Analysis

To help everyone better understand the practical application of these exchange rates, let’s take a specific example:

Suppose you go to a bank to exchange for US dollars, and the bank’s exchange rate is as follows:

  • 1 US dollar = 7.0 Chinese yuan
  • 1 US dollar = 7.2 Chinese yuan

Scenario 1: You sell US dollars to a bank

Assuming you have $1,000 and want to sell it to a bank, the bank will calculate using its buy rate

\[ $1,000 USD x 7.0 CNY/USD = 7,000 CNY \]

That means the bank will give you 7,000 RMB. The exchange rate is 买入汇率, as you are selling dollars to the bank.

Scenario 2: You buy US dollars with RMB

Assuming you have 7,000 RMB and want to exchange it for US dollars, the bank will calculate using the selling rate:

\[ $972.22 USD \]

In this case, 7000 RMB can be exchanged for approximately 972.22 USD. The exchange rate is 卖出汇率, as you are purchasing dollars from a bank.

Why is the buy rate different from the sell rate?

You may have noticed that the bank’s selling exchange rate (7.2 RMB/USD) is higher than its buying exchange rate (7.0 RMB/USD). This is because banks typically profit from foreign exchange transactions by exploiting this difference. In other words, banks make a profit by charging a higher selling rate and paying a lower buying rate.

For example, in the above case, the bank’s spread is:

\[ Selling exchange rate (7.2) - Buying exchange rate (7.0) = 0.2 RMB \]

This difference is the bank’s source of profit

Conclusion

  • The bank buys foreign currency from you at this exchange rate (the rate when you sell foreign currency)
  • The bank sells foreign currency to you at this exchange rate (the rate when you buy foreign currency)
  • The difference between the buying and selling exchange rates is the bank’s profit margin

Understanding these exchange rate concepts allows us to better know how much foreign currency we will receive or how much Chinese Yuan is needed to purchase it when exchanging currencies. Hopefully, this simple example helps everyone better understand the basic principles of foreign exchange rates!
