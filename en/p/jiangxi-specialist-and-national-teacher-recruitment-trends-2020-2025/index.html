2020-2025 Years: A Significant Reduction in Teacher Recruitment Trends in Jiangxi Province – Special Post Recruitment Numbers Plummeted from 6,617 to 32 (a decrease of 99.5%), while National Personnel Teachers decreased from 11,324 to 2,146 (a decrease of 81.1%). The proportion of core subjects (Chinese, Mathematics, and English) remained stable but declined in total volume; while the proportions of disciplines such as music, art, and physical education increased, the absolute numbers were limited (e.g., in 2025, they only recruited 2 people each). Policy-driven “One Out, One In” personnel adjustments tightened recruitment, with teacher resources leaning towards vocational training and remote areas; traditional primary and secondary school positions significantly reduced, and some subjects planned for 2025 were set to zero.

III. Data Description

  1. Data Source: Recruitment announcements and official statistics from the Jiangxi Provincial Department of Education and the Department of Human Resources and Social Security over the past years (actual recruitment numbers for 2020-2024, planned numbers for 2025).
  2. Subject Categories:
    • Special Post Teachers focus on subjects at rural primary education stages; only a small number of main subject positions are retained in 2025.
    • National Personnel Teachers cover all academic levels and have a more comprehensive curriculum, including preschool education and special education (due to data not being segmented, these were not listed separately).
  • Trend Analysis:
    • Sharp Decline in Total Numbers: The recruitment quota for Special Post Teachers decreased by 99.5% compared to 2020 in 2025, and the recruitment quota for National Personnel Teachers decreased by 81.1%, reflecting changes in population structure and adjustments in educational resources.
    • Hot vs. Cold Subjects:
      • The proportion of main subjects (Chinese, Mathematics, English) remained stable but declined year on year in terms of recruitment numbers. - The proportion of disciplines such as Audio-Visual Arts, Information Technology, etc., is rising, but the absolute number remains limited (e.g., only 2 people recruited in Audio-Visual Arts each year by 2025).
  • Some subjects (such as English) are facing suspensions due to declining student numbers.
  • Policy Background:
    • Tightening of Enrollment: Implementing a “withdraw one, fill one” policy, strictly controlling the addition of new positions, with external positions accounting for 68.6% in 2024.
    • Structural Adjustment: Teacher resources are leaning towards vocational education and remote areas, while recruitment in traditional primary and secondary schools has significantly decreased.

  3. Notes:

    • The number for some subjects (such as Information Technology) is planned to be 0 in 2025, and actual recruitment may be supplemented through adjustment.
    • Subject classifications may vary from year to year (e.g., “Moral and Rule of Law” merged with “Ideology and Politics”), please refer to the official announcement for the specific year.
